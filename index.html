Why Snowfall is a Groundbreaking Show

Snowfall is an American crime drama television series that follows the rise of the cocaine epidemic in Los Angeles in the 1980s. The series was created by John Singleton, Eric Amadio, and Dave Andron, and premiered on FX in 2017. The show chronicles the lives of several characters whose paths cross as the cocaine trade begins to infiltrate the streets of LA.

The Main Characters

Snowfall features a diverse cast with a mix of fictional and real-life inspired characters:

  • Franklin Saint - A young drug dealer who gets involved in the rise of the crack cocaine trade in LA.
  • Ruben Dos Santos - A CIA operative involved in the covert operations that aid the drug trade.
  • Louie - Franklin's aunt, who has a strong role in the family-run drug business.
  • Leon Simmons - Franklin's best friend and one of his main enforcers in the drug business.
  • Gustavo "El Oso" Zapata - A wrestler who becomes an enforcer in the cartel's operations.

The characters' intertwining stories reveal the complex dynamics of crime, family, and survival in the drug world.
